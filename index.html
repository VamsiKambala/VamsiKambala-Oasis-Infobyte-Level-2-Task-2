Bruce Lee

About Bruce Lee

Bruce Lee (Chinese: 李小龍; born Lee Jun-fan, 李振藩; November 27, 1940 – July 20, 1973) was a Hong Kong and American martial artist and actor.[2] He was the founder of Jeet Kune Do, a hybrid martial arts philosophy drawing from different combat disciplines that is often credited with paving the way for modern mixed martial arts (MMA). Lee is considered by critics, media, and other martial artists to be the most influential martial artist of all time and a pop culture icon of the 20th century, who bridged the gap between East and West. He is credited with promoting Hong Kong action cinema and helping to change the way Asians were presented in American films.

A life-long list of achievements:

  • 1940 - Born in San Francisco on November 27th.
  • 1941 - First film appearance.
  • 1963 - Chinese Gung Fu: The Philosophical Art of Self Defense by Bruce Lee first published.
  • 1964 - Presented with Karate Championship Award at the 1964 International Karate Championships for his "contribution to karate".
  • 1965 - Son Brandon Bruce Lee born February 1st.
  • 1966 - Appeared in The Green Hornet as Kato.
  • 1967 - Appeared on the United Cerebral Palsy Telethon to help raise money for victims of the disease.
  • 1969 - Daughter Shannon Emery Lee born April 19th.
  • 1972 - Appeared with son, Brandon, on Hong Kong TVB Operation Relief telethon to help raise funds to aid victims of a recent typhoon in Hong Kong; made a personal donation of $10,000 Hong Kong dollars. - Awarded a "Golden Horse Award" for Best Mandarin Movie for the film Fist of Fury.
  • 1973 - Died on July 20th.
  • 1975 - Tao of Jeet Kune Do first published.
  • 1993 - Hollywood Chamber of Commerce honored Bruce with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 1994 - Honored with a Lifetime Achievement Award at the 13th Annual Hong Kong Film Awards.
  • 1995 - Hong Kong post office released a postage stamp in honor of Bruce Lee.
  • 1998 - U.S. Senator Milton R. Young, President of U.S.T.A. and Jhoon Rhee, Vice President of U.S.T.A., honored Bruce for his international contribution to the martial arts.
  • 1999 - Profiled in Time magazine's Time 100: The Most Important People of the 20th Century under Heroes and Icons of the 20th Century.
  • 2000 - October 21st declared "Bruce Lee Day in San Francisco" by Mayor Willie L. Brown in honor and recognition of the "Little Dragon" in the year of the Golden Dragon.
  • 2004 - Received the EMMA (Ethnic Multicultural Media Academy) Legacy Award in London for his humanitarian achievement through the spiritual philosophy of martial arts which challenged racism and added substance to our lives; other recipients include Ghandi and Martin Luther King.
    • - Received a star on the Hong Kong Avenue of the Stars.
  • 2005 - A life size statue of Bruce Lee is unveiled in Mostar, Bosnia as a symbol of the fight against ethnic divisions. Bruce Lee is chosen by organizers as he is admired for his own ethnic struggles and accomplishments.
  • 2008 - A 26,000 acre nature preserve was dedicated to Bruce Lee as a memorial park in the Lee family ancestral home of Shunde, China complete with a Bruce Lee Museum, Martial Arts School, and the tallest Bruce Lee statue in existence at 18 meters high.

Unknown facts about Bruce Lee

  • Bruce Lee was a skilled musician, and played the piano, saxophone, and harmonica.
  • He was a philosopher and had a strong interest in Eastern philosophy, particularly Taoism.
  • Lee was also a talented artist and calligrapher.
  • He was a martial arts instructor, and taught celebrities such as Steve McQueen and James Coburn.
  • Lee was a cha-cha dance champion in Hong Kong in 1958.
  • He developed his own martial arts philosophy called Jeet Kune Do, which emphasizes adaptability and fluidity in combat.
  • Lee died at the young age of 32 due to a brain edema caused by a reaction to a painkiller he had taken for a headache.
  • The cause of his death was initially ruled as a misadventure, but later it was found that it was caused by an allergic reaction to the drug Equagesic.
  • Bruce Lee had a strong interest in nutrition and fitness, and advocated for a holistic approach to health and wellness.
  • He was also a writer and had written several books on martial arts and philosophy.
